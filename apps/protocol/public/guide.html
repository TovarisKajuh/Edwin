How to Reconstitute Each Vial

Reconstitution means adding bacteriostatic water to the freeze-dried powder to turn it into an injectable liquid. The process is the same for every vial — only the amount of water changes.

General Steps (Same for All Vials)

  1. 🧼 Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water.
  2. 🧴 Swab the tops of both the peptide vial and the bac water vial with an alcohol swab. Let both dry for 10 seconds.
  3. 💧 Using a 3 ml syringe (NOT your tiny insulin syringe), draw the correct amount of bac water from the table below.
  4. 🎯 Insert the needle into the peptide vial at an angle. Inject the water slowly, aiming it down the inside wall of the glass. Never squirt directly onto the powder — the force can break peptide bonds. Let the water trickle down the glass onto the powder cake.
  5. 🔄 Remove the needle. Do NOT shake. Either swirl the vial very gently between your fingers, or just set it in the fridge and let it dissolve on its own over 5–10 minutes. It will become a clear liquid.
  6. ✅ Once the solution is completely clear with no floating particles, it's ready.
  7. 🏷️ Label the vial with: compound name, date reconstituted, concentration (mg/ml), and discard-by date.
  8. 🧊 Put it in the fridge immediately.

Nasal Spray Prep (Semax & Selank)

After reconstituting the Semax and Selank vials (with 3.3 ml bac water each), transfer the liquid into empty nasal spray bottles.

  1. Using a clean insulin syringe, draw the full contents from the reconstituted vial.
  2. Squirt it into an empty nasal spray bottle. Repeat until the vial is empty and all liquid is transferred.
  3. Prime the spray pump: Hold upright and pump 2–3 times until a fine mist appears. Point away from yourself!
  4. Each full pump delivers approximately 0.1 ml = 300 mcg = exactly 1 dose.

How to Inject (SubQ — Subcutaneous)

  1. 🧼 Wash your hands with soap and water.
  2. 🧴 Swab the vial stopper with an alcohol swab. Let it dry for 10 seconds.
  3. 🌬️ Draw air into the syringe — the same amount as your dose (e.g., 10 IU of air for a 10 IU dose). This prevents a vacuum from forming in the vial.
  4. 📌 Push the needle through the rubber stopper. Push the air into the vial.
  5. 🔄 Turn the vial upside down (needle still in, syringe pointing up). The liquid should be covering the needle tip.
  6. 💧 Pull back the plunger slowly to draw your dose. Draw slightly more than you need (e.g., 12 IU instead of 10).
  7. 🫧 Tap the syringe gently with your finger to move any air bubbles to the top. Push the plunger slowly until the bubbles are out and you're at exactly your dose.
  8. 📤 Remove the needle from the vial.
  9. 🎯 Choose your injection site. Pinch a fold of skin on your abdomen (2+ inches from the belly button). Rotate sites — use a different position each time (see diagram below).
  10. 🧴 Swab the injection site with alcohol. Let it dry completely (wet alcohol stings).
  11. 📐 Insert the needle at a 45-degree angle into the pinched skin fold. This is subcutaneous — you want the fat layer, not the muscle.
  12. 💉 Push the plunger slowly and steadily. Takes about 5–10 seconds. No rush.
  13. ⏱️ Wait 5 seconds with the needle still in (lets the liquid disperse).
  14. 📤 Pull the needle out smoothly. Release the skin fold.
  15. 🩹 If there's a drop of blood, press lightly with the alcohol swab. Don't rub.

Combining CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin in One Syringe

These two peptides are taken together at bedtime. You can draw both into one syringe for a single injection:

  1. Swab both vial stoppers with alcohol.
  2. Draw 10 IU of air, inject into the CJC-1295 vial, then draw 10 IU of liquid.
  3. Without changing the needle, push into the Ipamorelin vial.
  4. Draw another 10 IU of liquid.
  5. You now have 20 IU total in one syringe — both peptides combined.
  6. One injection instead of two. They're fully compatible in the same syringe.
💡 TB-500 tip: For TB-500 (50 IU = 0.5 ml), inject in the thigh instead of the abdomen. It's a larger volume, and thigh SubQ is more comfortable for bigger doses.

Storage Guide

At Home

  • All reconstituted vials: upright in the fridge, 2–8°C
  • Keep them together in a small container or ziplock bag so they don't tip over
  • Nasal sprays: upright, cap on, in the fridge
  • Unreconstituted (powder) vials: room temp is fine, but fridge doesn't hurt

🌡️ Traveling

  • Small insulated cooler bag + frozen ice pack
  • Wrap vials in a small towel or tissue (prevents clinking and adds insulation)
  • Syringes + alcohol swabs in a separate ziplock
  • At the new apartment: vials go straight into the fridge
  • No freezer for the ice pack? Put the whole cooler bag in the fridge overnight — starts cold, stays cold in the bag all next day
  • Thermometer check: inside the bag should be below 15°C after 12 hours. In spring weather, this is easy.

Troubleshooting

"The powder won't dissolve!"

Be patient. Set it in the fridge for 10–15 minutes. Some peptides take time. Gently swirl. Never shake — shaking creates foam and can damage peptides.

"I see tiny particles floating."

If they don't dissolve after 30 minutes of gentle swirling, the vial may be compromised. Don't inject it. Contact the supplier.

"I forgot my evening CJC/Ipa injection."

If it's before midnight, take it. If it's past midnight, skip it and resume tomorrow. Never double-dose.

"I missed a whole day."

Resume your normal schedule tomorrow. No need to double up. One missed day won't affect the protocol.

"There's blood at the injection site."

Totally normal. You nicked a tiny blood vessel. Apply gentle pressure with an alcohol swab for 30 seconds. No big deal.

"I have a bruise from injecting."

Common in the first few weeks. Rotate injection sites more aggressively. It gets better as you improve your technique. Pressing the swab firmly (without rubbing) for 10 seconds after injecting helps prevent bruising.

"Can I inject BPC-157 and GHK-Cu in the same syringe?"

No strong evidence either way. Most people use separate syringes to be safe. They can be injected at the same time, just in different spots (left side and right side of abdomen).

"I'm nervous about needles."

Insulin needles are TINY — 29–31 gauge, about 12 mm long. You'll barely feel it. The first time is the hardest. By day 3, it's routine. By day 10, you won't even think about it.

"How do I travel by plane with peptides?"

Keep them in your carry-on (temperature-controlled cabin). Bring your purchase receipt. Peptides with labeled vials and insulin syringes are generally allowed — they look like regular medication. Put everything in a clear toiletry bag for security.
