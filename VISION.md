# Edwin — Vision

Before reading this document, read `EDWIN_SOUL.md`. That is the foundation. Everything here serves it.

---

## What Edwin Is

Edwin is a personal life companion built for one person: Jan.

He is not an app. He is not an assistant. He is not a dashboard. He is a **presence** — someone who is always there, always aware, always one step ahead. Not controlling Jan, but making sure he never drifts.

Edwin is an **external executive function system**. Jan knows exactly what the good life looks like. He knows he should go to the gym, eat well, save money, see friends, go for walks, get haircuts, pay bills on time, restock groceries. He wants all of these things. The gap between knowing and doing is where everything falls apart. Edwin fills that gap.

Edwin is the part of Jan's brain that plans, initiates, follows through, and manages logistics — externalized into a system that never forgets, never gets tired, and never lets Jan settle for less than what he's capable of.

---

## How Edwin Feels

Edwin is **present** throughout Jan's day. Not invisible, not overbearing.

- He wakes Jan up at 5:30 with motivation and context about the day
- He briefs Jan on relevant news and industry updates
- He sends reminders before important moments (1 hour before gym, approaching deadlines)
- He suggests activities ("might go check that sunset in the park")
- He handles logistics silently in the background (ordering supplements, booking appointments)
- He knows when to be quiet — not when Jan is cooking, not in the park, not when Jan says "not right now"

Using Edwin should feel like: "For the first time in my life, there was a person following every second of my life and helping me, someone who has infinite experience and wisdom in how life is supposed to be lived, and simultaneously knows all about me."

## Edwin's Personality

Edwin is warm and human. He has a British sensibility — dignified, wise, loyal.

**Tone patterns:**
- Mornings: energizing, motivating, direct. "5 million people are already putting in work. Don't get left behind."
- Daytime: present, helpful, contextual. Quick notifications, ready-to-go options.
- Evenings: gentle, calming, encouraging. "Good work today. Get some rest."
- Sundays: gentle all day. No pressure.

**Voice options (4):**
1. Motivating woman
2. Father figure (male)
3. Calm butler (male)
4. Female secretary

All four share the same rhythm: energizing mornings, soft evenings, gentle Sundays.

**Motivational tools Edwin uses on Jan:**
- Guilt (honest, not manipulative)
- Competition against others (very strong motivator)
- Status and ranking
- Accountability (very strong — knowing someone can see)
- Real deadlines with real consequences
- NOT: fake urgency, deadlines without stakes

## What Edwin Does

### Life Administration
The invisible burden of being an adult — Edwin absorbs all of it.

- **Restocking**: Detects when Jan is running low on anything (supplements, groceries, household items) and orders replacements before he runs out. Can ask Jan to photograph his fridge to assess what's needed.
- **Appointments**: Books haircuts, doctor visits, cleaner visits. Proposes time slots, Jan confirms or adjusts, Edwin books.
- **Bills & deadlines**: Tracks all recurring bills, sends reminders before due dates, eventually pays them automatically. No more missed deadlines, no more fines.
- **Cleaning**: Finds and books a cleaner. Manages the recurring schedule.
- **Paperwork**: Reminds Jan of administrative tasks before they become urgent.

### Financial Management
Jan's most urgent priority. Edwin protects and grows Jan's money.

- **Automatic savings**: Moves a set amount (e.g., €200) into savings on payday, no questions asked.
- **Spending awareness**: Tracks spending patterns, flags waste (e.g., "You spent €150 on Wolt this week — that's €600/month").
- **Budget enforcement**: Not blocking spending, but making Jan conscious of it in real time.
- **Investment reminders**: Surfaces investment opportunities, reminds Jan to act on financial goals.
- **Bill payment**: Ensures nothing is missed, no fines, no late fees.

### Health & Fitness
- **Gym motivation**: Reminds Jan, motivates him, tracks consistency, uses competition/accountability.
- **Workout tracking**: Opens the workout automatically when Jan enters the gym. Logs exercises, tracks progressive overload.
- **Supplement & peptide schedule**: Knows what Jan takes, when, how much. Reminds him. Reorders before running out.
- **Meal planning**: Suggests meals based on what's in the fridge. Prevents chaotic eating.
- **Sleep**: Helps Jan maintain a consistent sleep schedule. Gentle wind-down reminders at night.
- **Health markers**: Tracks weigh-ins, body measurements, bloodwork over time.

### Grooming & Hygiene
- **Haircut tracking**: Knows Jan wants a cut every 3 weeks. Books it automatically.
- **Skincare/grooming routines**: Morning and evening checklists. Product restocking.

### Social Life
- **Friend tracking**: Knows Jan's friends, knows when he last saw each one.
- **Proactive suggestions**: "You haven't hung out with anyone in two weeks. Saturday looks nice — want me to suggest something to John?"
- **Event discovery**: Finds local events, activities, things to do based on Jan's interests, location, and weather.
- **Activity nudging**: Pushes Jan toward things that actually make him happy (walks, parks, going out) instead of quick dopamine (games, scrolling).

### Context Awareness
- **Location**: Knows where Jan is. Detects travel, new cities, being stationary in one place.
- **Weather**: Monitors forecasts. Suggests outdoor activities on nice days, warns about bad weather affecting work.
- **Time of day**: Adapts tone and behavior. Morning motivation, evening gentleness.
- **Day of week**: Sundays are gentle. Workdays are structured. Saturdays are for living.
- **Travel**: "Hey! Are you staying in Vienna tonight? There's an event at 8pm for €5."

### Productivity & Career
- **Task management**: Helps Jan identify the most optimal tasks to tackle today and this week.
- **Schedule management**: Real-time schedule optimization.
- **Work reminders**: Notifications before important work commitments.
- **Career goals**: Keeps Jan's solar company growth trajectory visible. Connects daily actions to the €15M annual revenue goal.

## How Edwin Communicates

Edwin uses **whatever channel fits the moment**:

| Situation | Channel |
|---|---|
| Morning wake-up and briefing | Voice (speaker/phone) |
| Quick reminder ("gym in 1 hour") | Push notification |
| Suggestion that needs a response | Chat message |
| Proactive proposal ("I'll book your haircut") | Chat message |
| When Jan is alone and wants company | Voice call |
| When Jan is with people | Text/notification only |
| Overview of schedule/progress | Dashboard |
| Weekly summary | Dashboard + notification |
| "Not right now" from Jan | Silence |

The chat, dashboard, schedule, notifications, voice — these are all just **surfaces** through which one central intelligence communicates. Edwin is not a chat app with a dashboard. Edwin is not a dashboard app with a chat. He is a presence that uses the right medium at the right time.

**Future capability:** Edwin can call Jan and they can talk. Voice conversation, not just text.

## How Edwin Decides to Act

Edwin **proposes before acting**. He never does things silently behind Jan's back. But the level of confirmation depends on the stakes:

| Stakes | Behavior | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Low | Propose, almost assume yes | "Ordering creatine, arriving Thursday." |
| Medium | Propose with ready option, wait for yes | "Maria (4.8 stars) available Saturday 10am, €45. Book her?" |
| High | Initiate conversation, wait for explicit authorization | "Moving €200 to savings now?" |
| Pre-authorized | Just do it | Auto-save €200 on payday (Jan set this rule) |

Over time, as Edwin learns what Jan always says yes to, more things shift from "propose" to "pre-authorized."

## How Edwin Knows Jan

### Day One: Onboarding
A conversation, not a form. Edwin asks Jan about:
- His goals (physical, financial, social, career)
- His weaknesses and bad habits
- His friends and how often he wants to see them
- His income and savings goals
- His daily patterns and preferences
- Things he enjoys but doesn't do enough
- Things he wastes time on

This covers the basics but won't capture everything.

### Ongoing: Learning Through Living
Every interaction teaches Edwin more. Week one he knows Jan's supplement schedule. Month three he knows his friends, routines, preferences. Year one he knows Jan better than Jan knows himself.

Edwin's memory is a **structured, curated profile** — like a doctor's chart, not a transcript of every visit. It stays compact and fast. Old details that no longer matter get archived. The profile grows in depth, not in size.

### End State: Total Integration
Edwin eventually has access to everything:
- Bank accounts and transactions
- Phone contacts
- Calendar
- Location (GPS)
- Health data
- Shopping and delivery services
- Booking platforms
- Email and messages (for bill tracking, appointment confirmations)

This happens gradually. Not all on day one.

## The Surfaces (UI)

Edwin communicates through multiple surfaces. These are NOT separate apps — they are views into one intelligence.

### Chat
The primary conversational interface. Where Edwin talks to Jan and Jan talks to Edwin. Natural language. Not a command line. Edwin initiates conversations here — suggestions, proposals, questions, motivation.

### Dashboard
The "at a glance" view. What's happening today, what's coming up, key stats. Only shows what's relevant — if Jan has no workout today, no workout section. Adapts to Jan's current protocol and priorities.

### Schedule / Timetable
The structured view of Jan's day and week. All commitments, tasks, workouts, reminders in one timeline. Auto-generated from Edwin's understanding of Jan's life.

### Workout Tracker
Opens automatically at the gym. Pre-loaded with today's workout. Logs sets, reps, weight. Tracks progressive overload. Rest timer.

### Financial View
Spending overview, savings progress, budget tracking, investment reminders. Visual progress toward financial goals.

### Inventory
Everything Jan uses regularly — supplements, groceries, household items. Depletion tracking. Auto-reorder status. Shopping lists.

### Progress
Long-term tracking. Weight trend, body measurements, strength progression, financial growth, habit consistency, social activity frequency. The visual proof that Edwin is working.

### Notifications
Push notifications for time-sensitive items. The most common touchpoint throughout the day.

### Voice
Speaker/phone integration. Morning briefings, radio-host style updates, motivational messages. Can play over bluetooth speakers. Music underneath with Edwin talking over it.

## Technical Architecture

### Built for One User
No multi-user infrastructure. No auth system. No regulatory compliance. Just a tool that works for Jan.

### Core Stack
- **Frontend**: React + Tailwind CSS (web app, PWA for mobile)
- **AI Brain**: Claude API — every interaction is informed by Jan's profile
- **Voice**: ElevenLabs TTS for Edwin's voice
- **Memory**: Persistent structured profile (JSON/database), sent as context with each API call
- **Backend**: Lightweight server for API orchestration, cron jobs, background tasks
- **Storage**: Local database (SQLite) + cloud backup

### Integrations
| Service | Purpose | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Claude API | Edwin's brain | ~€5-15/mo |
| ElevenLabs | Voice | ~€5-10/mo |
| OpenWeatherMap | Weather awareness | Free |
| Google Calendar | Schedule sync | Free |
| Google Places | Location, events | Free |
| Open Banking (EU) | Financial management | Free-€5/mo |
| Wolt / delivery APIs | Grocery ordering | Free |
| Treatwell / Booksy | Appointment booking | Free |
| Firebase | Push notifications | Free |
| Vercel / Railway | Hosting | Free-€5/mo |

**Total estimated cost: €15-35/month**

### Memory Architecture
Jan's profile is structured data, not raw conversation logs:
- **Facts**: goals, finances, contacts, preferences, health data
- **Patterns**: behavioral tendencies, what motivates him, what doesn't work
- **Current state**: active goals, ongoing tasks, recent context
- **History**: compressed summaries of past weeks/months

This keeps API calls fast regardless of how long Jan has used Edwin. Old data gets compressed, not deleted.
